Session: v0.92 Channel Separation Breakthrough#

Date: 2026-02-23

Summary#

Major analytical breakthrough: discovered two cleanly separated causal mechanisms operating through different cost dimensions.

The Breakthrough#

  • GDP per capita interacts strongly with facility cost (pools, velodromes, golf courses): coefficients +5.8 to +6.6 per facility tier, p=0.006. No interaction with equipment cost (p=0.667–0.899).
  • Gini coefficient interacts with equipment cost (household participation barriers). No interaction with facility cost.
  • When both interactions included simultaneously, Gini gradient attenuates only 8% (from -0.499 to -0.455), confirming separability.

Interpretation#

Two distinct causal pathways: inequality operates through household cost barriers (talented kids from modest families can’t afford expensive sports), while absolute wealth operates through infrastructure provision (poor countries can’t build required facilities).

Paper Updates#

Abstract and introduction revised to highlight channel separation as key contribution. New sections documenting the horse race analysis. Interactive three-tab visualization created.

First, the post-Soviet transition is still actively distorting results through the 1990s — Atlanta 1996 has unified Germany for only its second Games, the CIS/successor states are still fielding inherited Soviet athletes, Yugoslavia is fragmenting.
