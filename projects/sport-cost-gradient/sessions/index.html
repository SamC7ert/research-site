Session: Norway's Olympic Gold and Silver Medals Across Eight Games

Session: Norway’s Olympic Gold and Silver Medals Across Eight Games Date: 2026-02-12 Chat link: Open in Claude Summary Extensive statistical investigation into whether societal equality predicts Olympic success per capita. Built on GSN Per Capita Cup data aggregating 4 summer plus 4 winter Olympics with standard 6-5-4 scoring. Used proper controls for GDP per capita and sophisticated analyses including Frisch-Waugh-Lovell partial correlations and multiple regression models. Key Findings Income equality shows a stronger independent signal than gender equality for predicting Olympic performance, particularly for women, even after controlling for wealth effects. A critical methodological error was discovered: when expanding to test for regional clustering effects (Galton’s Problem), Claude switched from the proper multi-Games GSN dataset to single-Olympics Paris 2024 data without flagging the change. All subsequent analyses of regional heterogeneity, democracy interactions, and communist legacy effects were compromised by this data switch. Outcome Collected all JSX files with embedded datasets. Created a markdown summary to brief a future session on redoing the analysis correctly, particularly focusing on whether regional clustering eliminates the income equality signal when using the methodologically sound 8-Games aggregated data.

Session: v0.91 Sport Type Decomposition

Session: v0.91 Sport Type Decomposition Date: 2026-02-19 Chat link: Open in Claude Summary Systematic decomposition of the “U-shape” pattern in equipment cost tiers, where Tier 2 (medium-cost) sports showed unexpectedly poor performance. Tested whether this represents a genuine cost mechanism or is driven by confounding factors. Key Findings Team sports (all concentrated in Tier 1) were not driving the cost gradient. Duel sports showed no cost sensitivity at all — cultural traditions dominate performance. Individual non-duel sports (timed/measured events like swimming, track, cycling) showed the cleanest cost mechanism. The equipment cost mechanism is robust when properly isolated from confounding sport characteristics. Methodological Insight Sport types operate through different mechanisms: team sports through institutional qualification barriers, duel sports through cultural tradition, and individual sports through household cost constraints. Proper decomposition is essential for isolating cost effects.

Session: v0.92 Channel Separation Breakthrough

Session: v0.92 Channel Separation Breakthrough Date: 2026-02-23 Chat link: Open in Claude Summary Major analytical breakthrough: discovered two cleanly separated causal mechanisms operating through different cost dimensions. The Breakthrough GDP per capita interacts strongly with facility cost (pools, velodromes, golf courses): coefficients +5.8 to +6.6 per facility tier, p=0.006. No interaction with equipment cost (p=0.667–0.899). Gini coefficient interacts with equipment cost (household participation barriers). No interaction with facility cost. When both interactions included simultaneously, Gini gradient attenuates only 8% (from -0.499 to -0.455), confirming separability.
