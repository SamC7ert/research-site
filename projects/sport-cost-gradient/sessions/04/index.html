Human#

Publication strategy: My recommendation for your situation:

  1. First: Post on SSRN as a working paper. Free, immediate, establishes priority, gets feedback.
  2. Then submit to Journal of Institutional Economics — it’s the perfect narrative fit (completing Geloso’s agenda), and if you have any Norwegian institutional affiliation, the APC is almost certainly covered by a read-and-publish agreement. If no affiliation, Cambridge offers waiver requests.
  3. Fallback: Journal of Sports Economics (free subscription publishing) or Social Science Quarterly (same). Writing style note: All these journals expect standard empirical economics format — abstract, introduction, literature review, data & methods, results, robustness checks, conclusion. Around 8,000-10,000 words. Tables with regression output. The tone in Geloso and Rewilak is fairly accessible compared to top-5 econ journals — no need for heavy formalism, but the econometrics need to be clean.

Do I need an affiliation to do the preprint? Is there any other formal demands?

Claude#

No, you don’t need an affiliation to post on SSRN. You can register as an independent researcher. There are no formal demands beyond having a paper to upload — no peer review, no fees, no institutional gatekeeping.

Practically speaking, you’ll want to include a contact email and a proper abstract. Listing an affiliation looks more credible, but “Independent Researcher” works fine and is common on SSRN. Many working papers there are from people outside academia.

For journal submission later, the situation varies. Most journals don’t strictly require an affiliation either, but it affects things like APC funding and how editors perceive your submission. Having a clean paper with solid methodology matters more than your letterhead, but you should be realistic that independent submissions face slightly higher scrutiny at the editorial triage stage.

One thing worth considering: if you have any connection to a Norwegian university or research institution — even a loose one like a visiting researcher arrangement — it can help both with credibility and with unlocking read-and-publish agreements for APCs.
