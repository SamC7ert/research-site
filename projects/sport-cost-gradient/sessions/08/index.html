Session: v0.91 Sport Type Decomposition#

Date: 2026-02-19

Summary#

Systematic decomposition of the “U-shape” pattern in equipment cost tiers, where Tier 2 (medium-cost) sports showed unexpectedly poor performance. Tested whether this represents a genuine cost mechanism or is driven by confounding factors.

Key Findings#

  • Team sports (all concentrated in Tier 1) were not driving the cost gradient.
  • Duel sports showed no cost sensitivity at all — cultural traditions dominate performance.
  • Individual non-duel sports (timed/measured events like swimming, track, cycling) showed the cleanest cost mechanism.
  • The equipment cost mechanism is robust when properly isolated from confounding sport characteristics.

Methodological Insight#

Sport types operate through different mechanisms: team sports through institutional qualification barriers, duel sports through cultural tradition, and individual sports through household cost constraints. Proper decomposition is essential for isolating cost effects.

For monotone countries, the strength metric is straightforward: the slope of a line through T1→T2→T3 shares. Steep positive = strongly tilted toward expensive sports. Steep negative = strongly concentrated in cheap sports.
