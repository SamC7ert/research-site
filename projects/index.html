Income Inequality, Sport Cost, and Olympic Performance

Working Paper Target journal: Journal of Institutional Economics Status: Draft v0.95 (February 2026) Abstract Geloso and Kufenko (2021) hypothesised that income inequality affects Olympic performance through sport-specific cost barriers, but could not test this due to the absence of reliable sport-cost data. We construct a two-dimensional cost classification – household equipment cost and training facility cost – for 41 Olympic sports and test whether inequality’s effect varies with cost, using a panel of 71 countries across seven Summer Games (2000–2024), scoring the top six finishers per event. ...

Spotting the Difference: Forward Price vs Expected Spot Price

Publication Journal: Energy Power and Risk Management (EPRM) — Electricity section Date: March 2001 Download PDF Summary This article examines whether forward electricity prices in the Nordic market (Nord Pool) are unbiased predictors of future spot prices, or whether systematic risk premiums exist. Drawing on data from the Norwegian and Nordic power exchanges, the analysis explores the theoretical relationship between forward and expected spot prices and tests whether the forward premium varies with seasonality, contract duration, and market conditions. ...

Finansieringsforutsetninger ved prosjektvurdering

Dissertation Institution: NHH Norwegian School of Economics (Norges Handelshøyskole) Programme: Høyere Avdelings Studium i Bedriftsøkonomi (doctoral-level programme) Supervisor: Professor Steinar Ekern Year: 1985 Front page (scan) Summary A discussion of different methods for evaluating the advantages associated with debt financing in project appraisal, illustrated with examples from the Norwegian continental shelf. The dissertation examines how financing assumptions — the choice between APV (Adjusted Present Value), WACC, and equity residual approaches — affect investment decisions in capital-intensive petroleum projects where tax shields, government participation, and project-specific debt structures create significant valuation differences depending on the method applied.
